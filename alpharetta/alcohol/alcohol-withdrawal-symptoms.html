CAUTION: Do Not Try to Withdraw from Alcohol on Your Own


Unsupervised Alcohol Withdrawal Can Be Deadly


If you or a loved one has an alcohol addiction or dependency, it’s critical to get medical supervision for the withdrawal process. Do NOT attempt to withdraw on your own. It’s not a matter of will power or “toughing it out”; there are real, physical dangers of going cold turkey.

Common withdrawal symptoms include:

  • Anxiety
  • Insomnia and fatigue
  • Shakiness
  • Headache
  • Rapid mood swings, from depression to excitability
  • Nausea, vomiting, and loss of appetite
  • Diarrhea
  • Heart palpitations
  • Sweating

Sometimes alcohol withdrawal leads to delirium tremens, which also includes:

  • Body tremors and seizures
  • Delirium and hallucinations
  • Disorientation and fear

Medically supervised detox ensures your safety and alleviates withdrawal symptoms.

It’s recommended to not only have doctors and nurses checking your vitals and administering medication, but also counselors to help you work through the psychological aspects. You also need a caring support staff to make sure you’re comfortable and supported while you detox.
