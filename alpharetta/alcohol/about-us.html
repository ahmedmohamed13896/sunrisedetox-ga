About Us

Daniel Lettenberger-Klein, Clinical Director

Mr. Lettenberger-Klein is a licensed marriage and family therapist who provides a wide range of services, including individual, couples, and family counseling. He specializes in working with individuals and families struggling with addiction and substance abuse issues. In his work with those who have experienced varying forms of trauma, such as physical and sexual abuse, Mr. Lettenberger-Klein uses Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR). He also has experience working with children and adolescents diagnosed with Asperger’s Syndrome. He uses a systems approach to therapy.

Previously, Mr. Lettenberger-Klein was the couples and family counseling supervisor for DISC Village, a regional provider of substance abuse treatment in Florida. He also maintained a private practice, serving individuals, couples, and families, and he was the family therapist for residential drug treatment centers serving adult women and adolescent males. Since 2012, he has been president-elect of the Tallahassee Association for Marriage and Family Therapy, and he is a Clinical Fellow with the American Association for Marriage and Family Therapy.

Mr. Lettenberger-Klein received his Bachelor’s Degree in Psychology from Bradley University in 2008, and he earned his Master’s Degree in Marriage and Family Therapy from Purdue University Calumet in 2011.

His professional affiliations and certifications include:

  • American Association for Marriage and Family Therapy
  • Georgia Association for Marriage and Family Therapy
  • Trauma-Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy
  • Crisis Intervention Trainer – Crisis Prevention Institute

Dr. Thomas Locke, Medical Director

Dr. Thomas J. Locke, III born in Vidalia, Georgia raised in SW Atlanta, did his undergraduate study at Princeton University.  He graduated from medical school at Howard University-where he also completed his internship.  He completed residency training at George Washington University.  Although Board Certification is not a guarantee of competency, Dr. Locke has accomplished this level of proficiency in 3 different specialties.  The process means that your physician and surgeon has received training and passed rigorous written and oral exams to achieve this level of certification.

Dr. Locke has been a board certified in internal medicine since 1981. In 1995, by virtue of clinical experience, teaching and other activities as well as passing rigorous written and oral exams Dr. Locke was certified in Emergency Medicine. Pursuit of diverse interests has led to also being certified in Aesthetics in 2009.

His practice philosophy centers on all aspects of personal image enhancement services-which includes treatment of addiction and related disorders.  Dr. Locke has extensive experience in treatment of substance abuse.  Since 2010, he has been certified in treatment of Opioid Dependence recognized by the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration. Dr. Locke takes great pride in bringing over 30 years of experience caring for complicated medical and surgical patients to the current practice.

Cathleen Coppola, MSW, CAP, ICADC, Lead Counselor

Cathleen is a Certified Addictions Counselor at Sunrise Detox in Alpharetta. In her role as Lead Counselor, “Cat” assists clients with every aspect of recovery, and fosters communication between clients and the many people in their lives who are critical parts of a successful recovery. Cathleen has prior experience in both private institutions and community health organizations.

A graduate of Barry University in Miami, Cathleen believes recovery is about learning how to become a whole, happy and healthy individual. As with all counselors at Sunrise Detox, Cat offers an “open door” policy for recovery counseling.
