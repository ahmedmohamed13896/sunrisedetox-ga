How Opiate Treatment Works


1. Admit You Have a Problem

Recovery starts when you admit you have a problem and pick up the phone to get help. This is the most important part of the entire process. But it's also critical that you seek proper treatment because detoxing from opiates without medical supervision is so agonizing, most people can’t do it alone and they relapse. When you call, a trained addiction advisor will check your insurance and answer any questions so you know exactly what to expect when you get here. We can even pick you up from anywhere in the Stirling Metro area -- free of charge.


2. Check In

You'll be greeted by a caring intake specialist when you arrive at our clean, comfortable facility.  You'll get help checking in and getting settled in your room.  A nurse will check your vitals to start your medical detoxification treatment and make sure you're comfortable right from the start.


3. Relax and Recover

A team of caring, qualified doctors and nurses will monitor your physical detoxification to ensure a safe, comfortable withdrawal from opiates. Group and individual counseling is available but there's also plenty of free time to relax and watch TV in your room or in the common area. Delicious and healthy meals are prepared daily by the chef on staff. You also have access to a chiropractor and massage chair to aide in your healing and relaxation.
