CAUTION: Do Not Try to Withdraw from Benzos on Your Own

Unsupervised Benzo Withdrawal Is Terrorizing – and Dangerous

If you or a loved one has a benzo addiction, it’s critical to get medical supervision for the withdrawal process. Do NOT attempt to withdraw on your own. It’s not a matter of will power or “toughing it out”; there are real, physical dangers of going cold turkey.

The most common withdrawal symptoms are anxiety, panic attacks, and seizures.

Medically supervised detox ensures your safety and alleviates withdrawal symptoms.

It’s recommended to not only have doctors and nurses checking your vitals and administering medication, but also counselors to help you work through the psychological aspects. You also need a caring support staff to make sure you’re comfortable and supported while you detox.
